Hedy Lamarr

Hollywood actress contributed to the development of modern wireless communication

Hedy Lamarr had an interesting and full of events life:

  • 1914 - Born in Vienna, Austria-Hungary.
  • 1920s - Brought to Berlin by producer Max Reinhardt to train in the theater.
  • 1930s - Raises as a European star from extra role in Money on the Street (1930) to the lead role in No Money Needed (1932).
  • 1933 - Plays the lead role in the film Ecstasy that gained was considered an artistic work in Europe but was banned in America and Germany. On August 10 Lamarr marries Friedrich Mandi, an Austrian military arms merchant and munitions manufacturer.
  • 1937 - Separates with Mandi and arrives in Paris. Hedy meets Louis B. Mayer who persuades her to change her name to Hedy Lamarr.
  • 1938 - Louis B. Mayer brings Hedy to Hollywood and promotes her as the "world's most beautiful woman".
  • 1938-1945 - Under MGM contract Hedy plays the role of a archetypal glamorous seductress in numerous films developing a successful Hollywood actress career. Boom Town (1940), Comrade X (1940), Come Live with Me (1941), H.M. Pulham, Esq. (1941), Crossroads (1942), to name a few. From the films White Cargo (1942) became one of the top-billed movies and huge hits in Hedy's career.
  • 1940-1945 - Hedy learns that radio-controlled torpedoes could be easily javmmed and set off course. She comes to an idea of creating a frequency-hopping signal that could not be tracked or jammed. With this idea, she contacts her friend, composer George Antheil who synchronizes a miniaturized player-piano mechanism with radio signals.

  • 1942 - Hedy Lamarr and George Antheil are granted with a patent for the developed invention.
  • 1945 - Leaves MGM and forms production company with Jack Shertok.
  • 1946-1947 - Produces The Strange Woman (1946) and Dishonored Lady (1947) that went over budget and had not had commercial success.
  • 1949 - Stars as Delilah in Samson and Delilah (1949), the film embodying her biggest success and winning two Oscars.
  • 1950-1958 - With her career coming into decline, Hedy continues to play in films through 1958 with the last thriller - The Female Animal (1958).
  • 1953 - Becomes a naturalized citizen of the United States.
  • 1960 - Receives the star on the Walk of Fame for the contribution to the motion picture industry.
  • 1966 - Hedy's autobiography, Ecstasy and Me, is published. Hedy sues the publisher claiming that much of the autobiography was fictional.
  • 2000 - Dies at the age of 85. Hedy had six marriages and three children.
